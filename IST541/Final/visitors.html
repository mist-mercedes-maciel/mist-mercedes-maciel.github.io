Welcoming Visitors to the Park


Starts at the Gate


The most important times to remember are starting the tour on time and gething the tour out on time.

For an efficient and timely start to your tour, it is recommended that you, D1, arrive at the Highway 1 gate 30 minutes before tour scheduled time while  D2 stay in the parking lot area.

Have an entrance and parking scheme to facilitate the manage the visitors arrival. For example, some visitors wait in line for the park to open becouse they think there is coast access. Therefore, it is recomended that you ask 'Are you here for the three hour-guided tour to the Lightstation?' If their answer is yes, proceed to explain the five Ts

Try to get cars off highway as quickly but safely as possible.






The 5 Ts


Due to the location and tour structure, explaining the 5 Ts at the gate is very important. The 5 Ts are a consice and quick way to interprete the rules and structure of the tour. This helps visitors in their decision to commit to the tour or turn around before they get access to the park.


The five Ts are:

  • TOUR- This is a guided-walking tour with a 360 elevation and two staircases
  • TIME- The tour last three (3) hours.
  • TOILETS- Visitors have access to portable restrooms located in the parking area and at the top of the rock (about two hours later).
  • TOLL- Visitors pay for the tour once they reach the Visitors Center at the top of the rock, $15 adults, $5 age 6-17, and no toll for children under 5 years of age.
  • TEMPERATURE- Let visitors know that it can get cold and very windy at the top of the rock, so layers of clothes are recommended.

Other rules specific to Pt. Sur State Historic Park are:

  • No dogs or pets in the park (including leaving them in cars at the parking area) except for service animals.
  • No access to beach.
  • No food or smoking.
  • All visitors at the Lightstation must be escorted between parking area and Highway gate (at the begining and at the end of the tour) by a volunteer or docent.
  • All natural and cultural resources at the park are protected.
  • No wheels (strollers, wheelchairs*, mobility devices*) are allowed at the Lightstation.

accessible *For ADA access, visitors must contact State Parks at least 4 weeks prior to visit to arrange for transportation from the parking lot to the top of the rock for tour.







Safety Talk at the Parking Area


While good timing is essential for a successful tour, safety is imperative, so once you have reach the tour's capacity (or is five minutes before the scheduled tour time) close the Highway 1 gate, and drive back to the parking area. Here you will welcome the visitors and start your tour. But, before you can start the tour, you must give the visitors the safety talk.

  • Visitors must stay on the pavement and watch their step while walking up the road.
  • Don't go after anything  that goes over the edge. It does get prety windy which could cause hats, scarves or sunglasses to fly off the visitors persona.
  • Do not run while going up or down the road.
  • Do not get ahead of docent, and stay with the group.

Once you are finish the safety talk, you can begin the tour.
