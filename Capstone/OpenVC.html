Open the VC

Prepare the Visitors Center for a Tour

Objective: In this section you will learn the series of task necessary for an effective Visitors Center experience.

   Before the day of the tour, you will receive communication from the D1 regarding plans for the team to arrive at the park. For example, some docents like or need to carpool to the park, some prefer to drive together as a caravan, and others prefer to meet at the park.

   If you are meeting the rest of the tour team at the park, remember to arrive one hour before the scheduled start time of the tour, and follow the 'Gates Procedure' as explained in the Tour Management I training.

   When you sign up to work the Visitors Center, you will be expected to complete a series of task that will prepare the VC for visitors. The task process is similar in both locations (Lighthouse VC and NavFac VC), but this section will focus on the task process at the Lighthouse VC because it is more extensive and complex here.

   The following is a suggested ordely list of these tasks. Once you become more familiar with the operation of the VC, you will create your own routine to accomplish them.


  1. De-humidifiers, Air filter, and Blinds.

When you arrive, turn-off de-humidifiers. There are two de-humidifiers in the VC. One is located in the main merchandise room (by the mugs display), below the window. The second one is located inside the docent closet, in the Macon room.

Turn-on the air filter. The air filter is located in the main merchandise room by the hanging rack (oposite side to the de-humidifier).

Open the blinds of all windows to let the natural light in.



  2. Check for Updates.

Towards the back of the VC bulding, in the Macon room, you will find the docents' closet. Inside the docents' closet you will see the radio cabinet and other essential tour materials such as the tour backpacks for the D1 and D2 conducting the tour. Here, you will also locate the second de-humidifier mentioned above.

Head over the docents' closet and check for any posted announcements, any updated information regarding the operations of the lightstation tours or any other important news.

Sign in the tour chart by adding the date of the tour you are working next to your name.


anchor At the NavFac, you will find any updates posted in the Docent's Admin room.



  3. Radios, Phone, and Jump-Starter.

In the docent closet you will find the radio cabinet. Here you will locate the docent radios (walkie-talkies) and the radios charging port, the tour backpacks for D1 and D2, and additional tour materials. In this cabinet, you will also find the cash box (more about the cash box in the next module).

Check operation of all docent radios. Ensure that radios are off and charged or changing while they are in the radio port. Take a charged walkie, turn it on and keep it with you at all times; this is your main form of communication with the rest of the team. The state radio always goes with the docent that will be letting visitors in at the Highway 1 gate.

Check VC telephone for dial tone. If the landline telephone does not have a dial tone, report the outage to the Chair or Vice Chair of volunteers asap. AT&T personal cellphones work at the rock.

Finally, check the CAT Jump-Starter to ensure it is changed - recharge if necessary.


anchor At the NavFac, the radios, radio port and cash box are located in the Docent's Admin room closet.



  4. Set up the  Square (Point of Sale System).


The next module is dedicated to learn how to set up the Square, but it is recommended to complete this procedure when the rest of the tour team heads down to Highway 1 gate to welcome visitors.   

 This process is the same in both VC locations.



  5. Check the Beverage Bar.

Check the Beverage Bar:

  1. Turn on the hot water dispenser switch (ensure the light turns on).
  2. Water level in the hot water dispenser should be full.
  3. Check that the beverage stock (coffe, tea, sugar, napkins, etc) are organized and sufficient for a tour.
  4. Clean the top of the counter if necessary.
  5. Check the water cooler dispenser (it should be over 1/4 full).
  6. Place seed money ($2.00) in donation jar (more on this in the next module).

anchor There is no beverage bar at the NavFac, but the donation jar is located on the brochures table.



  6. Merchandise and Brochures


Check for low stock or messy displays.


  • Restock merchandise and straighten the shelf if needed.
  • Restock and straighten the brochures display if needed.


  7. Flags and Volunteer Banner.

Raise the U.S. and the California flags if the wind permits.

Using bungee cords, attach the volunteer banner to the fence outside the VC.


anchor At the NavFac, D1 or D2 take care of raising the flags and placing any signs outside the building.



  8. Monitor.

While the rest of the team are leading the tour, you will have time to finish the tasks mentioned above, so there is no need to feel rush!

Pay attention to the tour whereabouts (D1 and D2 will give you radio updates of their location as they approach the VC), once they get closer to the VC, start the videos in the Macon room and in the Communications display room.

Periodically monitor the highway gate and beaches for traspassers or other unusual activity. Contact CA Parks/Ranger or Ranch Security if needed.

warning If you leave the VC, while others are touring the park, lock the from door, and keep the keys and your docent radio with you.
