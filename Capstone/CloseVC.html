Closing the Visitors Center After a Tour

Objective: In this section you will learn the series of task necessary to close the Visitors Center after the tour.

   Closing the Visitors Center process is similar in both locations (Lighthouse VC and NavFac VC). This section will focus on the tasks at the Lighthouse VC because the process is more extensive and complex.

   The following is a suggested sequential list of tasks that you do to close the VC.

    Once you become more familiar with the operation of the VC, you will create your own routine to accomplish them.


  1. Close the Square.


Once the tour heads down to the parking area, you may begin the VC closing process. The first task is to close the Square System.

  • Count the money in the Cash Drawer and check for donations.
  • End the Cash Drawer in the Square and Print the Sales Report.
  • Fill out the Tour Envelope and Deposit it in the safe box.

warning Do not leave the Cash Box short! If the till is short, leave the Tour Envelope short and write a note.


   Share with D1 the total sales and if the Cash Box balanced or not.



  2. Radios and Tour Materials.


When the tour docents return to the VC, ensure that the docent radios (walkie-talkies) and the State radio are off and properly placed in the changing port.

Confirm with D1 and D2 that the tour backpacks are complete and that keys are attached.


anchor Remember that at the NavFac, the radios, radio port and cash box are located in the Docent's Admin room closet.



  3. Beverage Bar.


Clean the Beverage Bar:

  1. Turn off the hot water dispenser switch (ensure the light turns off).
  2. Water level in the hot water dispenser should be full.
  3. Clean the top of the counter if necessary.
  4. Check the water cooler dispenser (it should be over 1/4 full).

anchor There is no beverage bar at the NavFac, but wipe clean the submarine demo table (if it was used).




  4. Coordinate with D1 and D2 .



Even though you are the VC docent, all volunteers are great team players and often help with the closing duties. C oordinate with D1 and D2 with regards to:

  • Locking of the toured buildings and setting alarms.
  • Lowering the flags and storing them in the garage.
  • Removing and rolling the volunteer banner.

anchor At the NavFac, these type of tasks are performed by D1 and D2.



  5. Merchandise and Brochures.



Check for low stock or messy displays.


  • Restock merchandise and straighten the shelf if needed.
  • Restock and straighten the brochures display if needed.


 6. Dehumidifiers and lights.

Close the blinds of all windows.

Turn off the air filter. The air filter is located in the main merchandise room by the hanging rack (oposite side to the dehumidifier).

Turn on dehumidifiers. There are two dehumidifiers in the VC. One is located in the main merchandise room (by the mugs display), below the window. The second one is located inside the docent closet in the Macon room.

  • Make sure to leave the docent closet door open!

Turn off all lights.



  Notes


Make any notes in the docent log, preservation crew's log, and Out-of-Stock log as needed.

For any serious or safety issues contact the Chair and Vice Chair of Volunteers ASAP.
